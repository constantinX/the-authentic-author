The War for Children

Published 20 March 2022

In eastern Ukraine, children are living in constant fear of death or injury from mines and explosives. Many have been left with lifelong disabilities as a result of the conflict. UNICEF estimates that around a third of all recorded deaths and injuries among children during the conflict were caused by mines and explosives. This is an absolutely horrifying statistic.

Children in war zones are living in incredibly difficult and dangerous conditions. They are constantly exposed to violence and death, and they live in fear of being injured or killed themselves. This is an incredibly traumatic way for any child to grow up, and it is nothing short of heartbreaking.

It is vital that we do everything we can to help children in war zones. They deserve to have a safe and happy childhood, free from the fear of violence. We must do everything we can to protect them and to ensure that they have a future. There are many organizations that are working to help children in war zones, and they need our help. We can donate money to these organizations, or we can volunteer our time to help them with their work. Every little bit helps, and these children desperately need our help.

The international community has a responsibility to children to prevent and end these grave violations,” said Fore. Among the grave violations against children are the following: Children recruited, conscripted and used by armed forces and armed groups The use of children as soldiers continues to be a major issue in conflict zones. The UN verified more than 8,000 cases of recruitment and use of children by armed forces and armed groups in at least 10 countries since 2010. More than 2,400 of the verified cases were in Nigeria, with at least 1,200 in the Central African Republic. Children killed and maimed in conflict Children are often killed or maimed in conflict, whether caught in the crossfire or deliberately targeted. The UN verified more than 23,000 cases of children killed and maimed in conflict since 2010. The majority of the verified cases were in Afghanistan, Iraq, Nigeria, South Sudan, Syria, and Yemen. Nearly 15,000 verified cases of children maimed were in Afghanistan, Iraq, Syria and Yemen. Sexual violence Sexual violence against children, particularly against girls, is widespread in conflict zones. The UN verified more than 8,000 cases of sexual violence in at least 16 countries since 2010. A large number of the verified cases were in the Democratic Republic of Congo.
