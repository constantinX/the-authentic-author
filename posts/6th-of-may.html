Friday, 6:15 am

Starting my new routine. No Alcohol, waking up at 5:45 am, Meditation 10 min, Morning Pages 15 min, getting ready, preparing the food for the girls, bringing my older daughter to school. I am not sure if i want to go to the gym right then. I wouldn't see my youngest one until that very afternoon. And then i'd only see her in the evenings. Is there an option that I go at 6 am to the gym, be back at 7 am and then bring the kids to school? I would have to get up at 5:15 am, do 10 min of Meditation, 5:25 am then 15 min of morning pages, 5:40 am make breakfast and coffee. 6 am go to the gym, 6:10 am arrive, 6:15 am warm up, 6:20 am workout, 7 am go back, 7:10 am be back home to bring my girls to school. I don't know if this is sutainable.

I am still nervous. I am shaky from time to time. I have to work on my bulletproofing. I have to work on controlling my emotions. They cannot make me do dumb things. What is the plan?

I will be creating from within an emotional safe place, I will create an psychological safe place. For that, I will study communication to learn how to lead and conduct a conversation. I have ordered a book that hopefully gives me some insights. Next, I will control my emotional response. I will control it in such a way that I will not get triggered by anything. Nothing shall shake me. Nothing shall be able to bring me out of balance. Lastly, I will shift my identity. I have to really identify what she needs. I need to be happier. I need to be the positive beacon in her world. I do not have to run around smiling everyday, but she needs to learn that she can expect always a positive outcome when interacting with me.

What is emotional safety?

  • Respect boundaries and consent.
  • Pay attention to your nonverbal communication.
  • Be an active listener.
  • Practice transparency.
  • Give your partner the benefit of the doubt.
  • Foster accountability and follow through.
  • Consider couples or relationship therapy.

What is psychological safety?

  • Show your partner you're engaged.
  • Let your partner see you understand.
  • Avoid blaming to build trust.
  • Be self-aware‚Äîand demand the same from your partner.
  • Nip negativity in the bud.
  • Include your partner in decision making.
  • Be open to feedback.
  • Champion your partner.

Kill my inner judge! Kill my inner critic! Never ask why questions that could be interpreted as an attack on her motives, ideals or convictions. Begin conversations with opening her up. A gentle touch, be aware of your own non-verbal communication. Be kind. Smile. Are you in a state that you can take on this conversation? Are you calm enough?

Today, I have my first therapy session. I am not sure what to think. Can he help me, guide me? I am ambivalent. I am not sure. Am I going too fast? I signed up for the gym. I signed up for Salsa classes. I need to follow through with these changes. I need to embrace the change. I will make me uncomfortable.

Changes I am working on right now:

  • Be kind.
  • Don't judge.
  • Be emotionally bulletproof
  • Start Meditation
  • See the world through her eyes. Be openminded
  • Be Positive
  • No Alcohol

I have to be kind. I have to be gentle. I feel that I don't get through to her. I have a hard time opnening her up.

10:07 am

I have been struggling with this lingering, hidden sexual abuse that I experienced. I will call it as such, even though a part of me still does not know if it is true or if I wanted it too. I was young. Very young. Too young to know. I am struggling with expressing my emotions too. As a young boy, one was never encouraged to speak about it. I learnt that "we do not know pain", I learnt that "we do not fail". There is a lot of work that I need to do. I know that. At the same time, I have to not only better my emotional pains, I also have to save my relationship, my family. I have to become everything at the same time. I am quite overwhelmed. I stopped drinking. I will not touch a beer or other alcohol again. I hope this will help me a lot. I need to give her space, need to do things right. Don't make any mistakes. I am so scared. Can I turn it around? Can I provide the safety that she needs? Can I provide the support that she needs? I have to respect her boundaries. If she needs time, I shall not run upstairs to her. It is a little tricky with the kids. I think I have a right to be with them when we are here.

I need to learn about non-verbal communication. Mine and hers. I have to be more pulled back and give her space when she needs it. I want to learn how to dance. I wanted this for quite some time now. I never went through with it. I want to take her out for dancing. I want her to feel joy. I want to open myself up for joy. I need to practice smiling. It is a little tough to smile consistently. It is a little tough to strengthen that muscle and make it a habit.

Everything is turning green outside.
